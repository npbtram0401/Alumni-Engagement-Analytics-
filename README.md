# University of Greenwich Alumni Engagement Analytics

This project is based on a real-world business scenario where I take on the role of a Social Media Manager at the University of Greenwich Alumni Office. The main objective is to analyze alumni data and develop data-driven strategies to convert alumni into donors and sponsors (DS) through social media channels, specifically Facebook and Twitter.

The project focuses on three donor tiers (Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3) and evaluates engagement across four key content themes: acquisition, retention, corporate social responsibility, and university news. By integrating CRM data (demographics and behavior) with social media performance data, the goal is to understand audience characteristics, identify engagement patterns, and optimize communication strategies.

A data-driven project analyzing donor and alumni engagement using CRM, Facebook, and Twitter data fully conducted in Excel. It covers data cleaning, exploratory analysis, and interactive dashboard development to uncover insights on audience demographics, content performance, and engagement behavior.

Step 1: Data Cleaning & Preparation - Cleaned and structured raw data in Excel (handling missing values, inconsistent formats, and data types) to ensure accuracy and consistency.

Step 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) - Used Excel functions and charts to analyze patterns in demographics, content performance, and engagement behavior.

Step 3: Dashboard Development – Built interactive dashboards in Excel to visualize insights across CRM and social media platforms.

Step 4: Insights & Recommendations – Interpreted key findings to provide actionable recommendations on content strategy, audience targeting, and optimal posting timing.

# CRM Analysis – Key Insights
•	Tier 2 donors (£100–£499/year) are the largest contributor group → strong potential for upgrading to higher tiers
•	Donor base is highly concentrated in the UK (~90%) → opportunity to expand engagement with international alumni
•	Alumni aged 50+ show the highest contribution levels → indicates strong loyalty and financial capacity
•	Younger alumni segments are under-engaged → require long-term nurturing strategies
•	Majority of donors are employed → suggests stable income links to higher donation likelihood
•	No significant difference across professional sectors → donations are not industry-dependent
•	Peak login activity occurs mid-week (Tuesday & Thursday) → optimal timing for communication and campaigns
